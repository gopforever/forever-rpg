Combat System

Contents

The combat system in Forever RPG is tick-based — combat resolves in discrete time intervals called ticks. The speed of these ticks is configurable via the Combat Speed slider in Settings. The full combat engine is implemented in js/combat.js.

Tick-Based Combat edit

Every combat tick, the game processes all attacks, spells, ability cooldowns, damage-over-time effects, and regeneration. Actions do not resolve in real time — they wait for the next tick.

Combat Speed

The Combat Speed slider in Settings controls the duration of each tick:

  • 500 ms — Maximum speed (fast-paced, 2 ticks per second)
  • 1000 ms — Default recommended speed
  • 5000 ms — Minimum speed (slow, 1 tick per 5 seconds — great for AFK play)

Slower tick rates are useful if you want to review each combat action in the log before the next round resolves, or for AFK grinding sessions.

What Happens Each Tick

Each combat tick processes, in order:

  1. Player character melee attacks against their current target
  2. Ability triggers and spell resolutions
  3. Enemy attacks against party members (based on aggro)
  4. Damage-over-time (DoT) and heal-over-time (HoT) effects
  5. HP/Mana regeneration for sitting characters
  6. Death checks — characters or enemies at 0 HP are processed
  7. Loot distribution (if auto-loot is on) for defeated enemies

Initiating Combat edit

Enemy Grid

The center panel shows an enemy encounter grid — a visual list of all enemies currently in your zone's combat encounter. Click an enemy portrait to select it as the current target.

The Pull Button

Click Pull to engage combat with the selected enemy. The tank character (or the first party member capable of it) rushes in and initiates the encounter. All other party members join based on their class role and abilities.

Auto-Engage

If enemies are present in your camp and your party is not in combat, they will auto-engage after a short delay. This makes AFK grinding possible — set your camp, configure your hotbar, and let the party work.

Combat Buttons edit

Four primary combat control buttons appear in the combat area:

ButtonEffect
Stop Fighting Immediately halt all combat actions. Your party stops attacking. Useful to avoid accidentally pulling extras or to recover before engaging again.
Sit Characters sit down to rest, drastically increasing HP and Mana regeneration per tick. Characters cannot attack while sitting. Click again or Pull to stand up.
Pull Engage the selected enemy. Your tank initiates combat. If no enemy is selected, a random available enemy in the zone is targeted.
Set Camp Designate your current location as your camp point. Enemies from the zone will spawn near your camp. Dying players respawn at the city; camp persists.

Sitting & Mana Regeneration edit

Mana-using classes (all Hybrids, Casters, and Priests) regenerate mana passively each tick. While sitting, mana regeneration is dramatically increased. Sitting also increases HP regeneration for all classes.

Characters cannot act — attack, cast spells, or use abilities — while sitting. They automatically stand up if attacked. A common strategy is to sit between pulls to recover resources quickly, especially for Clerics who burn mana keeping the party alive.

Aggro & Taunt edit

Each enemy maintains an aggro list — a ranked list of characters it prefers to attack. The character at the top of the aggro list receives the enemy's attacks.

Taunt (Warrior, Paladin, Shadow Knight) is the primary tool for controlling aggro. When a warrior taunts, they immediately move to the top of the enemy's aggro list. Healers generate aggro by casting heals; heavy DPS characters generate aggro with damage. The tank's job is to Taunt frequently to keep enemies focused away from the healer.

Spell Hotbar edit

The spell hotbar appears in the combat area below the enemy grid. It contains slots for each of your selected character's abilities. Click an ability slot to activate it immediately (if off cooldown and enough mana is available).

Abilities are defined per class in js/classes.js, with each ability having:

  • Mana Cost — Mana consumed on use (0 for physical abilities)
  • Cast Time — Time in ms before the spell resolves
  • Recast Time — Cooldown in ms before the ability can be used again

Combat Log & Zone Chat edit

Below the combat area, tabs let you switch between:

  • Combat Log — A running log of all combat events: attacks, misses, crits, spells, and deaths.
  • Zone Chat — Live chat shared with other players currently in your zone. Powered by the persistent ghost/player system (see Zone Chat).
  • Loot — Items dropped by recently defeated enemies, available for pickup if Auto-Loot is off.

Loot System edit

When an enemy is defeated, it drops loot based on its internal loot table (defined in js/enemies.js). Drops can include weapons, armor, accessories, consumables, and currency.

  • Auto-Loot ON — Loot is automatically distributed to the first party member who can use/carry it.
  • Auto-Loot OFF — Loot appears in the Loot tab; click items to pick them up manually.

Currency (gold, silver, copper) is always looted automatically and added to your wallet. See Currency for denomination details.

Death & Revival edit

Death

When a character's HP reaches 0, they die. Dead characters:

  • Cannot attack, cast spells, or use abilities
  • Enter a ghost state — their ghost persists in the world as visible to other players
  • May be revived by a Paladin's Revive ability or a Cleric's Resurrection
  • If not revived in combat, they respawn at the hub city Qeynos with partial HP

Ghost State

Dead player characters remain in the world as ghosts for up to 72 hours after the player logs off (stored in localStorage key foreverRPG_ghosts). Other players can inspect these ghost characters via the Ghost Inspect modal, which shows the ghost's equipment and stats.

Revival

In-combat revival requires a Paladin or Cleric with the appropriate ability. Out-of-combat, characters can return to Qeynos and recover at full HP at the next zone load. Experience penalty on death is noted in the combat log.

Camp System edit

Clicking Set Camp marks your current position as a camp. The camp system controls which enemies from the zone's encounter table will spawn near your party. Different camps within a zone may pull from different enemy sub-groups — experienced players use camps to target specific rare spawns.

Rare enemies (e.g., named monsters with unique loot) have a low spawn chance (typically 8% per pull, defined as rareSpawnChance in js/zones.js). Group encounters (multiple enemies at once) also have a configurable spawn chance per zone.

Items & Equipment

Contents

The item system in Forever RPG covers everything from the simple Short Sword you start with to legendary Named rarity drops from the Plane of Fear. Items are defined in js/items.js and displayed with rich tooltips powered by js/tooltips.js.

Item Categories edit

Every item has a type field that determines how it behaves:

TypeDescriptionExamples
weapon Equippable in the primary or secondary slot. Has dmg (damage), delay (attack speed in tenths of a second), and weaponType (slash/pierce/blunt/hand_to_hand). Short Sword, Dagger, Hatchet, Staff of Shielding
armor Equippable in body slots. Provides armor class (ac) and optional stat bonuses. Class and race restrictions apply. Leather Tunic, Chain Hauberk, Plate Breastplate, Ringmail
consumable Single-use items that provide a temporary effect when activated. Includes potions, food, and drink. Minor Healing Potion, Bread, Water Flask
bag Containers that expand inventory capacity. Has a capacity (number of slots) and optionally a weightReduction percentage. Small Bag, Traveler's Pack, Extraplanar Trade Satchel
tradeskill Components used in crafting and trade skill recipes. Not currently equippable. Ore, Pelts, Vials of Various Substances

Item Rarity edit

Items come in four rarity tiers, indicated by name color in the UI:

RarityColorDescription
Common⬛ Grey/WhiteBasic gear available from vendors or low-level drops. No special properties beyond base stats.
Magic🔵 BlueHas at least one magical property — stat bonuses, resists, or a worn/use effect. The backbone of mid-game progression.
Rare🟤 Orange/BrownPowerful items with multiple bonuses. Often from named enemies or deep dungeon encounters.
Named🔴 RedThe finest items in Norrath — typically Lore and/or NoDrop. Dropped by boss-tier named enemies.

Item Flags

  • Lore — You may only carry one copy of a Lore item at a time. Attempting to pick up a second gives an error.
  • NoDrop — The item cannot be traded to other players, sold on the Market, or banked. It stays with the character who looted it.

Drag-and-Drop Equipping edit

Forever RPG supports full drag-and-drop interaction for managing equipment, powered by js/drag.js:

  • Drag an item from your Inventory panel to an equipment slot to equip it.
  • Drag an equipped item to an inventory slot to unequip it.
  • Drag between inventory slots to reorganize your bag.
  • Drag an item to the Bank panel (when in Qeynos) to deposit it.
  • Drag from the Bank to Inventory to withdraw an item.

Class and race restrictions are enforced on drop — if your character cannot use the item, the drop is rejected.

Item Tooltips edit

Hovering over any item — in your inventory, equipment slots, bank, or market — shows a detailed tooltip rendered by js/tooltips.js. The tooltip includes:

  • Item name (colored by rarity) and Lore/NoDrop flags
  • Slot, type, and weight
  • For weapons: Damage, Delay, and weapon type
  • For armor: Armor Class value
  • Stat bonuses (STR +5, STA +10, etc.)
  • Resist bonuses (Magic, Fire, Cold, Poison, Disease)
  • Worn/Use effect name
  • Class and race restrictions
  • Flavor text (in italic)

Loot & Drops edit

Enemies drop loot when defeated. Each enemy in js/enemies.js has a defined loot table specifying which items can drop and at what probability. Higher-rarity items drop less frequently.

The Combat System article covers the Auto-Loot toggle and manual loot pickup in detail.

Bank & Market edit

Bank

The Bank (accessible from the City panel in Qeynos) provides unlimited item storage. Items in the bank are safe from loss on death and persist across all sessions. Use the bank to store rare drops, trade skill materials, or items you plan to sell later.

Market

The Market is the player-driven economy of Norrath. List your items for sale at any price in gold/silver/copper. Other players can browse listings and purchase items. Market data is stored in localStorage (key foreverRPG_market) and shared through the ghost system.

Currency edit

Forever RPG uses the classic three-tier currency system of Norrath:

CurrencySymbolConversion
CoppercBase unit. 10 copper = 1 silver.
Silvers10 silver = 1 gold.
GoldgHighest denomination. 1 gold = 100 copper.

Your current wallet balance (gold / silver / copper) is always visible in the top bar. Currency looted from enemies is automatically added to your wallet regardless of the Auto-Loot setting.

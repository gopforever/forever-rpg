Classes

Contents

Classes define a character's role in combat, the abilities they learn, their stat growth, and which races can play them. There are 16 classes in Forever RPG, organized into four archetypes: Melee, Hybrid, Caster, and Priest. Full ability definitions are in js/classes.js and js/skills.js.

Archetypes edit

Every class belongs to one of four archetypes:

  • Melee — Pure fighters with no mana pool. Highest physical output and survivability. Use disciplines and combat skills.
  • Hybrid — Blend melee ability with magic. Versatile but specialized; often fill two roles simultaneously.
  • Caster — Intelligence-based spellcasters. Highest single-target magic damage; fragile without group protection.
  • Priest — Wisdom-based support. Essential healers and utility casters; keep the party alive.

Class Stats & Leveling edit

Each class has a list of primary stats — the 2–3 stats that receive +2 per level. All other stats receive +1 per level. This means melee classes like the Warrior naturally grow their STR/STA/AGI faster than INT or WIS.

Hit points are calculated from STA using the hpPerSTA multiplier, which increases at level 50. Warriors have the highest multiplier (4.5/6.0), making STA especially valuable for them. Casters have a low multiplier (2.0/2.8) and rely on staying out of direct combat.

Mana System edit

Pure melee classes (Warrior, Berserker, Monk, Rogue) have no mana pool — their abilities are based on timers (recast cooldowns) rather than a resource.

All other classes use one of two mana stats:

  • WIS-based — Paladin, Ranger, Beastlord, Cleric, Druid, Shaman. Higher Wisdom = larger mana pool.
  • INT-based — Shadow Knight, Wizard, Magician, Necromancer, Enchanter. Higher Intelligence = larger mana pool.

Mana regenerates over time while out of combat, and is accelerated by using the Sit command.

Party Roles edit

A balanced party of 6 typically includes:

  • 1 Tank — Usually Warrior or Paladin. Taunts enemies and absorbs damage.
  • 1 Healer — Usually Cleric or Druid. Keeps the party alive.
  • 1 Crowd Control — Enchanter (Mesmerize) or Bard (Lulling Melody). Handles extra enemies.
  • 2–3 DPS — Any combination of Rogue, Wizard, Monk, Ranger, etc.
  • 1 Support/Buffer — Shaman (Slow + buffs) or Bard (Haste songs).

Smaller parties work well too — even a solo character can progress through lower-level zones. The party system is managed by js/party.js.

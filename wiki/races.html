Races

Contents

Races are the foundational choice made during character creation. Each race provides a unique set of base statistics and restricts which classes that character may pursue. All characters begin in Qeynos — The City of Honor, regardless of their racial homeland.

Stat values are sourced from js/races.js in the game repository.

Overview edit

There are 14 playable races in Forever RPG, inspired by the classic Norrath setting of EverQuest. Race selection is permanent for a character, so choose carefully. The primary stats are:

  • STR (Strength) — Melee damage and encumbrance capacity
  • DEX (Dexterity) — Attack speed, ranged accuracy, and proc rate
  • AGI (Agility) — Evasion and armor class contribution
  • STA (Stamina) — Hit points; each point contributes HP based on class
  • WIS (Wisdom) — Mana pool for Priest classes (Cleric, Druid, Shaman, Paladin)
  • INT (Intelligence) — Mana pool for Caster classes (Wizard, Magician, Necromancer, Enchanter, Shadow Knight)
  • CHA (Charisma) — NPC faction, merchant pricing, and Enchanter effectiveness

Race Selection Tips edit

Best Melee Races

For pure melee damage and tanking, Ogre (STR 130, STA 127) and Troll (STR 108, STA 119) offer unmatched physical power. Ogres also have a notable resistance to being stunned from the front. Barbarians offer a good balance of STR and STA with a slightly wider class selection.

Best Caster Races

Erudites lead with INT 107, making them the ideal choice for Wizard, Magician, Enchanter, and Necromancer. High Elves shine for WIS-based casters (Cleric) with WIS 95 and INT 92. Dark Elves are a strong second choice for INT-based casters with INT 99.

Best Agile Races

Halflings, Wood Elves, Half Elves, and Gnomes all offer DEX/AGI scores in the 90–95 range, making them excellent Rogues, Rangers, and Bards.

Most Versatile Race

Humans are the only race that can play every class, including all three Priest classes and all four Caster classes. Their perfectly balanced stats (75 across all attributes) make them a safe pick when you're undecided on a class.

How Race Stats Affect Gameplay edit

Race base stats combine with class base stats at character creation. As characters level up, each stat gains +1 (or +2 for a class's primary stats) per level. This means the starting stat gap between races narrows over time — but racial identity remains meaningful at all levels because the initial advantage carries forward.

The bonusStats values on each race in js/races.js are reserved for future display use and do not currently apply to character stat calculations.

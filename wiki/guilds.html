Guilds & Community

Contents

Forever RPG features a rich community layer built around guilds, parties, and a persistent ghost-player system that makes the world feel alive even when you're the only one online. Community features span js/guilds.js, js/party.js, and js/players.js.

Guild System edit

Guilds are player-organized groups that persist across sessions. Joining a guild gives your characters a guild tag displayed next to their name and contributes their kills and XP to the guild's cumulative totals for the Rankings.

Seed Guilds

Five guilds are pre-seeded into the world and available for players to join:

Guild NameTagMotto (MOTD)Theme
Crusaders of Norrath[CoN]"Honor above all."Paladin / good-aligned focus
The Dark Covenant[TDC]"Power through shadow."Shadow Knight / evil-aligned
Arcane Society[AS]"Knowledge is power."Spellcasters & scholars
Ironforge Brotherhood[IFB]"Steel and sweat."Warriors & melee fighters
Order of the Vale[OtV]"Nature guides us."Druids, Rangers & nature classes

Ghost players from the community are distributed across these guilds (3–12 members each), giving the guilds life even without manually enrolled players.

Guild Ranks

Every guild uses a four-tier rank system:

  1. Recruit — New member, limited permissions
  2. Member — Full member with access to guild bank (future feature)
  3. Officer — Can invite new members and manage Recruits
  4. Leader — Full control; can promote, demote, and disband the guild

Creating a Guild

To create your own guild, open the Guild tab in the Qeynos City panel and click Create Guild. You'll be prompted for a guild name and tag. The founding character becomes the Leader. Your new guild appears in the guild browser for other players to join.

Joining a Guild

Browse available guilds in the Guild tab, select one, and click Join Guild. You'll enter as a Recruit. Guild data is saved to localStorage under the key foreverRPG_playerGuilds.

Guild Stats

Each guild tracks cumulative statistics across all members:

  • Total Kills — Combined enemy kills by all guild members
  • Total XP — Combined experience earned
  • World Firsts — Number of World First achievements earned by guild members
  • Member Count — Active and ghost members
  • Founded — The date the guild was created

Party System edit

The party system (js/party.js) manages your group of adventuring characters. Unlike guilds (which are persistent social organizations), a party is your active combat group that you directly control.

Party Roster

The Party Roster panel on the left sidebar shows all current party members with:

  • Character name, race icon, and class icon
  • Current level
  • HP bar (current / max)
  • Mana bar (current / max, if the class has mana)
  • Status indicator (alive, sitting, dead)

Click a party member in the roster to select them as the active character — the right sidebar updates to show that character's stats, equipment, abilities, and inventory.

Party Size

A party can contain up to 6 characters. You can adventure with a smaller party or even solo (1 character), but larger parties are necessary for the most dangerous zones. Characters are added during character creation and cannot be added mid-session without creating a new character slot.

Combat Roles

See Party Roles in the Classes article for recommended party compositions. The party system automatically routes enemy attacks to the character with the highest aggro, and the Taunt ability lets tanks redirect attacks away from healers and DPS.

Who's Online edit

The Who's Online panel (left sidebar) shows a list of ghost players currently present in the game world. These are characters from other players who have logged into the game — their data is shared through the localStorage ghost system.

Each entry in Who's Online shows:

  • Character name and class icon
  • Current zone
  • Level
  • Guild tag (if in a guild)
  • Online/offline status (ghosts persist up to 72 hours after last login)

The ghost system updates every 30 seconds (TICK_SECONDS = 30) while you are active. Ghost data is stored in localStorage under the key foreverRPG_ghosts, with tick timestamps in foreverRPG_ghostTick. After 72 hours offline (MAX_OFFLINE_TICKS = 8640 ticks), a ghost expires and is removed from the world.

Ghost Inspect edit

The Ghost Inspect feature allows you to examine another player's character in detail. Open it by clicking a ghost in the Players tab in Qeynos or from the Who's Online panel.

The Ghost Inspect modal shows:

  • Character name, race, class, level, and guild
  • Full equipment set with item names and tooltips
  • Character stats (STR, DEX, AGI, STA, WIS, INT, CHA and derived stats)
  • When the character was last seen online

Inspecting a ghost player earns you the People Watcher achievement.

Zone Chat edit

Zone Chat is a shared text channel for all players currently in the same zone. It appears as a tab in the combat log area of the center panel. Zone chat messages from ghost players appear alongside your own, creating a sense of a populated world.

Zone chat messages are part of the ghost data — active players' chat contributions are included in their ghost snapshots and visible to others in the same zone. Seeing 10 zone chat messages earns the Social Butterfly achievement.

World Feed edit

The World Feed is a global activity stream visible in the center panel. It shows notable events happening across Norrath:

  • Players entering or leaving zones
  • Level-up announcements
  • World First achievement announcements
  • Named enemy kills by other players
  • Guild formation announcements

The World Feed uses ghost data to generate realistic-looking activity from the broader player community, making the world feel active and alive even in a single-player session.
